4-DOF Robotic Arm with Sensor-Based Hand Motion Control

Designed and built a glove-controlled robotic arm that mirrors hand gestures in real time. IMU, flex, and FSR sensors on the glove drive a 4-DOF arm via PWM, achieving pick-and-place of stacked objects in under 30 seconds — 2× faster than the project requirement.

Technical Details

Sensor & Control System

  • Integrated IMU, flex, and FSR sensors on STM32F411RE to drive 4 servos via PWM in real time.
  • Implemented IIR filtering and regression pipelines to suppress sensor noise and stabilize servo mapping.
  • Interrupt-driven architecture for responsive sensor-to-actuation control with minimal latency.

Mechanical Design

  • Designed full arm structure in Onshape with parametric CAD, fabricated via FDM 3D printing.
  • Executed multiple design iterations to resolve assembly constraints, servo clearance, and alignment issues.
  • Custom gripper design optimized for reliable grasp of stacked objects.

Power Electronics

  • Engineered a dual buck converter power distribution system with per-servo decoupling.
  • Eliminated voltage drop under high-load conditions (>6A stall current), ensuring stable multi-servo operation.

Challenges & Solutions

  • IMU drift under dynamic motion — resolved with bias/scale correction and ellipsoid fitting calibration.
  • Flex sensor non-linearity — corrected using regression model trained on measured deflection data.
  • Servo jitter under load — eliminated through per-servo decoupling capacitors and PWM signal cleanup.
